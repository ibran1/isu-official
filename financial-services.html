Frequently Asked Questions

What is the duration of the ITM Skills University MBA in Hospitality Management program?

The MBA in Hospitality Management program at ITM Skills University has a duration of two years and four semesters.

What are the eligibility criteria for admission to the MBA in Hospitality Management program?

You must have completed a bachelor's degree from a recognized university or institution to be eligible for the program.

What are the core subjects covered in the MBA in Hospitality Management program?

The core subjects covered in the program include Food Production Administration, Food and Beverage Service Operations, Accommodation Operations, MICE management, Function Catering Management, Financial Management, Marketing Management, etc.

Are there practical training or internships included in the program?

Yes, ITM Skills University emphasizes practical learning and offers internships, industry projects, and practical training opportunities. These experiences provide students real-world exposure to the hospitality industry and enhance their practical skills and industry connections.

What are the career prospects after completing an MBA in Hospitality Management?

Graduates of the program can pursue various leadership and managerial roles in the hospitality industry, including hotel manager, resort manager, event manager, food and beverage manager, revenue manager, hospitality consultant, and hospitality entrepreneur.

Does ITM Skills University provide placement assistance for MBA in Hospitality Management graduates?

Yes, ITM Skills University typically offers placement assistance to its students. The university's placement cell organizes recruitment drives, job fairs, and campus placements to connect students with potential employers in the hospitality industry.

Does Masters' Union have a cut-off for GMAT, GRE or CAT?

Masters’ Union follows a holistic evaluation approach for admissions. Our selection is based on a combination of your application, essays, exam scores and interview performances. However, if you feel that your GMAT/CAT/GRE scores are not competitive enough, we encourage you to appear for our own internal entrance examination, the MUBAAT. We do not accept NMAT scores.
